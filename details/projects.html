Crop Counter for Brazilian Nonprofit

Python, various LLM APIs, Google Sheets scripts

As a UIUC Grainger Career Scholar, I worked in a group of four to research the viability of using AI to count crops.

This was part of a partnership with Toca, a Brazilian nonprofit organization that supports family farming. Many family farmers never learn basic math, reading, or technology skills, which makes them reliant on others to calculate the amount of castor beans that they've produced.

My group of 4 researched different AI tools and methods to determine the easiest and most accurate way to calculate the mass of a pile of beans (like the one in the image), and presented our findings and analysis to the organization.

Challenges:

Finding an AI model that could recognize and count so many items; especially since the beans are natural shapes rather than all looking the same, it was difficult for models to recognize them. We addressed this by expanding our search for models, as well as by taking different approaches such as estimating the area a pile took up and using that for our calculations.

Getting an estimate for the actual weight of the beans,; the nonprofit didn't have any weight estimates, and the weight of castor beans varies based on factors that we couldn't access, such as humidity. We addressed this by creating a tool that allowed the nonprofit to weigh one batch of beans and input the result, which then generated statistics on the accuracy of each of our solutions.
